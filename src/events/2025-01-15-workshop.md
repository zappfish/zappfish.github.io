**Workshop goals and content**
* discussed the key challenges in accessing, reusing, and integrating zebrafish toxicology and phenotype data
* introduced the project goals in creating an open, searchable, community-driven zebrafish toxicology phenotype atlas to improve data consistency, integration and alignment with  cross-species data standards
* reviewed the potential pitfalls of the atlas, with proposed mitigation strategies  
* discussed the role of sematic technologies and ontologies in atlas development and data interoperability
* introduced project governance and proposed how the community will engage and contribute
* conducted hands-on exercise to review the images of the most common, strangest, and hardest-to-annotate zebrafish phenotypes
